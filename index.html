Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 26, 2026

Our Commitment

FocusShield was built with a privacy-first mindset. We believe that your browsing habits and productivity data should remain yours and yours alone. This policy explains how we handle data and why we require certain permissions.

Data Collection

FocusShield does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers.

We do not track your browsing history, we do not use cookies for tracking, and we do not have a backend server that stores your information. All data used by the extension stays on your local device.

Local Storage

We use chrome.storage.local to save your settings, such as:

  • Your custom list of blocked websites.
  • The preferred block duration for the timer.
  • The "Secure Lock" timestamp (to prevent settings modification).

This data is stored locally in your browser profile and is never shared with us or any third party.

Permissions Usage

To function correctly, FocusShield requires the following permissions:

Host Permissions ()

This allows the extension to detect when you are visiting a site on your block list (like YouTube Shorts) and inject the focus timer.

Scripting

Used to inject the timer UI and the blocking logic into the web pages you've chosen to block.

Storage

Used to remember your settings and block preferences locally.

Third-Party Services

FocusShield does not use any third-party analytics or advertising services. The extension is 100% ad-free and tracker-free.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or the extension, feel free to contact us via the Chrome Web Store support tab or open an issue on our official repository.
